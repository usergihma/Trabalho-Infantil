Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is child labor and why is it prohibited?

Child labor is any form of work performed by children or adolescents that compromises their physical, emotional and educational development. Brazilian legislation prohibits child labor to protect children's rights and guarantee their access to education.

2. How do I find my first job?

Getting your first job can be challenging, but there are several ways to get started. Some steps include:

  • Create a good resume, even without prior experience.
  • Search for internship or trainee programs.
  • Participate in courses and training to acquire new skills.

Additionally, many job sites and agencies offer specific opportunities for those entering the job market.

3. How can I report child labor?

If you are aware of any situation involving child labor, it is important to report it to protect the rights of the child or adolescent. You can report it through the following channels:

  • Dial 100 - Child and Adolescent Abuse and Exploitation Reporting Service.
  • Public Ministry of Labor.
  • Local Police Stations and Guardianship Councils.

4. What can I do to combat inequality in the workplace?

Inequality in the labor market can manifest itself in many ways, such as wage discrimination, lack of opportunities for women, black people, LGBTQIA+ people, among others. To combat this, it is important to:

  • Report discriminatory practices.
  • Seek education and training to occupy better positions.
  • Support inclusion and diversity initiatives in companies and organizations.
